How characters grow and change ○
│  | ↣ interesting: V:tM has TWO progression systems — one MECHANICAL (XP)
│  |   and one NARRATIVE (Generation/diablerie). You can grow through play
│  |   but the CEILING only changes through the game's darkest act.
│
├──→s Character creation: who you start as ○ {V20}
│   │  | Priority-allocation across categories. Clan choice first.
│   │  | ↣ interesting: the system forces TRADE-OFFS — you can't be good at
│   │  |   everything. 7/5/3 means one category is WEAK. Identity through limitation.
│   │
│   ├──→s Concept/Clan/Nature/Demeanor: identity foundation {V20}
│   │      | ↣ interesting: Nature vs Demeanor is a BUILT-IN duplicity mechanic.
│   │      |   Every character has a public mask and a private truth.
│   │      |   Vampires are liars BY CHARACTER SHEET DESIGN.
│   │      └── ✶? Single-trait system: one Archetype that is both public and private
│   │             (what if vampires CAN'T hide who they really are?)
│   │
│   ├──→s Attribute/Ability allocation: mechanical foundation {V20}
│   │      | 9 Attributes (3 Physical/Social/Mental), 30 Abilities (Talents/Skills/Knowledges)
│   │
│   └──→s Freebie points: customization layer {V20}
│          | 15 points to break the template. Different costs per type.
│          | ↣ interesting: freebie costs INCENTIVIZE specific choices — Backgrounds
│          |   at 1 pt are cheapest, Disciplines at 7 pt are expensive. The economy
│          |   pushes you toward social/political traits, not raw power.
│
├──→s Experience progression: gradual growth ○ {V20}
│   │  | XP per session/story. Costs scale with current rating.
│   │  | ↣ interesting: in-Clan Disciplines cost LESS than out-of-Clan (×5 vs ×7).
│   │  |   Your BLOOD determines what's easy to learn. Nature vs. nurture, mechanically.
│   │  |   Also: Attributes are expensive (×4), Abilities cheap (×2).
│   │  |   You can learn NEW things easily but IMPROVING what you are is hard.
│   │
│   └── ✶ Deterioration system: skills decay without practice ▲
│          | Over decades/centuries, unused Abilities fade. Elders forget how to
│          | use modern technology. Knowledge becomes obsolete.
│          | ↣ interesting: immortality as FORGETTING — you gain centuries but lose details
│          | ⊕ prior art: some V:tM supplements hint at this, Requiem's memory loss
│
├──→s Generation: the hard ceiling ○ {V20}
│   │  | Generation limits EVERYTHING: blood pool, blood/turn, Attributes, Disciplines.
│   │  | Only changes through diablerie or starting Background.
│   │  | ↣ interesting: the only PERMANENT power increase in the game requires
│   │  |   CONSUMING ANOTHER VAMPIRE'S SOUL. There is no clean path to the top.
│   │
│   └──→s Diablerie ◆: cannibalism as advancement △ {V20}
│          | ↣ interesting: THE central moral-mechanical tension of progression.
│          |   Want more power? Murder and devour. Don't want to? Accept your ceiling.
│          |   The game offers exactly ONE shortcut and it costs everything.
│          └── ✶! XP-equivalent moral cost: spend permanent Humanity dots instead of XP ▲
│                 | Buy power directly with morality points. 1 Humanity = N XP.
│                 | Explicit corruption-as-currency without the murder step.
│                 | ↣ interesting: soul-selling as character advancement mechanic
│
├──→s Embrace: creating new vampires ○ {V20}
│      | Sire drains, feeds blood back. One Generation higher.
│      | ↣ interesting: every new vampire is WEAKER than their parent.
│      |   Entropy is built into vampiric reproduction. Each generation
│      |   is a dilution. The species is DECLINING by design.
│
├──→s Nature/Demeanor Archetypes ◆ {V20}
│      | ~40 personality types. Nature = true self (regains Willpower when fulfilled).
│      | Demeanor = public mask (no mechanical benefit).
│      | ↣ interesting: Willpower regeneration is tied to BEING TRUE TO YOURSELF.
│      |   If your Nature is Monster, you regain Willpower by being monstrous.
│      |   If it's Caregiver, you regain by helping others. The game REWARDS
│      |   consistency of character, not morality.
│      └── ✶? Archetype that CHANGES over time (evolving Nature)
│
└──→s Ghoul progression ○ {V20}
       | Weaker than vampires, capped by domitor's Generation.
       | Three types: Vassals (bonded servants), Independents (free agents),
       | Revenants (hereditary, self-producing vitae).
       | ↣ interesting: revenants are BORN ghouls — hereditary servitude.
       |   Families bred as tools for centuries. The darkest extension of blood-as-hierarchy.
