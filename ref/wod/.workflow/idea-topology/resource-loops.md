## TREE

```
Resource economy: what vampires spend, gain, and convert ○
│  | ↣ interesting: vampires have ONE primary resource (blood) that does EVERYTHING.
│  |   This creates constant tension — heal vs fight vs boost vs feed others.
│  |   The single-currency economy is what makes the game's choices agonizing.
│  | ⊕ seen in: mana/spell slots (D&D), but blood is MORE central because it's
│  |   also your FOOD, your MONEY, and your DRUG
│
├──→s Blood Pool ◆: the primary resource △ {V20}
│   │  | Stored vitae. You ARE your blood pool. Everything costs blood.
│   │  | ↣ interesting: blood is simultaneously health, mana, currency, and food.
│   │  |   No other RPG collapses this many resource types into one.
│   │
│   ├──→s Blood spending: what blood buys
│   │   ├──→v Power activation: Discipline fuel (1-3+ per use) {V20}
│   │   ├──→v Physical boosting: +1 Attribute per blood per turn {V20}
│   │   ├──→v Healing: 1 per bashing/lethal HL, 5+5WP per aggravated HL {V20}
│   │   ├──→v Maintenance: 1 to rise each evening {V20}
│   │   └──→v Social investment: creating ghouls, blood bonds {V20}
│   │
│   ├──→s Blood gaining: feeding
│   │   ├──→v Mortal feeding: 1 BP/turn, Kiss causes ecstasy {V20}
│   │   │      | ↣ interesting: feeding is INTIMATE and PLEASURABLE for the victim.
│   │   │      |   Predation disguised as seduction. The horror is that victims ENJOY it.
│   │   ├──→v Animal feeding: less efficient, socially acceptable {V20}
│   │   ├──→v Kindred feeding: possible but politically dangerous {V20}
│   │   └──→v Diablerie: ultimate feeding — consume soul, lower Generation {V20}
│   │          | ↣ interesting: cannibalism-as-advancement. The ONLY way to permanently
│   │          |   increase your power cap. Murder as character progression.
│   │
│   ├──→s Generation: the blood pool's ceiling ◆ {V20}
│   │      | 13th Gen: 10 BP, 1/turn. 8th: 15 BP, 3/turn. 4th: 50 BP, 10/turn.
│   │      | ↣ interesting: Generation is a HARD CAP that can only be changed by
│   │      |   diablerie or the Generation Background (starting lower). Elders are
│   │      |   more powerful because they LITERALLY have more blood to spend.
│   │      |   Economy-as-hierarchy.
│   │      ├── ✶ Diminishing-returns blood pool: more blood = less efficiency per point ▲
│   │      │      | Elders have MORE but get LESS per unit. Bloated, wasteful. Neonates
│   │      │      | are lean and efficient. David vs Goliath economics.
│   │      │      | ↣ interesting: inverts the elder advantage — youth as efficiency
│   │      │      | ⊕ prior art: many video game scaling systems, diminishing returns in economics
│   │      └── ✶! Blood pool that DEGRADES over time: stored blood loses potency each night ▲
│   │             | Hoarding is impossible. You MUST feed regularly or your reserves rot.
│   │             | Fresh blood is always better than old blood.
│   │             | ↣ interesting: forces engagement with the world — no hermit vampires
│   │
│   └──→s Blood Bond (Vinculum) ◆: blood-as-control mechanism △ {V20}
│       │  | Three drinks from same vampire = supernatural love/devotion.
│       │  | ↣ interesting: LOVE as a weapon. The bond feels REAL to the thrall.
│       │  |   Is chemically-induced devotion less valid than "natural" emotion?
│       │  |   The game's darkest social mechanic.
│       │  | ⊕ seen in: love potions (fairy tale), but permanent and darker
│       │
│       └──→v Vaulderie ◆: communal bond as Bond countermeasure △ {V20}
│              | Shared blood ritual creates DISTRIBUTED bonds (weaker, many-directional).
│              | Prevents strong individual bonds.
│              | ↣ interesting: the Sabbat's answer to Blood Bond tyranny is
│              |   EVERYONE being slightly bonded to EVERYONE. Distributed addiction.
│              └── ✶! Voluntary bond-breaking ritual: sacrifice X to sever a Blood Bond △
│                     | What would you trade to be free? A Discipline dot? A year of unlife?
│                     | ↣ interesting: freedom has a price, literally — economic liberation theology
│
├──→s Willpower ◆: the secondary resource △ {V20}
│      | Spent for automatic successes, resist Disciplines, overcome frenzy.
│      | Regained by fulfilling Nature Archetype or Storyteller award.
│      | ↣ interesting: Willpower regain is tied to PERSONALITY — you get stronger
│      |   by being true to yourself. Identity-as-resource-regeneration.
│      | ⊕ seen in: Fate points, Inspiration (D&D 5e), but tied to personality type
│
├──→s Health system ○ {V20}
│      | 7 health levels with wound penalties. Three damage types.
│      | Bashing/lethal healed by blood. Aggravated = slow and expensive.
│      | ↣ interesting: aggravated damage (fire, sunlight, claws) is the ONLY thing
│      |   vampires truly fear. Creating a three-tier damage system means most
│      |   combat is trivial but SPECIFIC threats are existential.
│
├──→s Virtues ◆: internal moral resources △ {V20}
│      | Conscience/Self-Control/Courage (Camarilla) OR Conviction/Instinct/Courage (Sabbat).
│      | Rolled to resist degeneration, frenzy, Rötschreck.
│      | ↣ interesting: your MORALITY has a numerical value that you roll against.
│      |   Virtue isn't a choice — it's a STAT. You can fail to be moral.
│      | ⊕ seen in: Pendragon's personality traits (mechanical morality)
│      └──→v Conviction/Instinct: Sabbat moral replacement {V20}
│             | Conviction = ideological certainty (replaces Conscience).
│             | Instinct = predatory reflex (replaces Self-Control).
│             | ↣ interesting: the Sabbat doesn't lack morality — they have DIFFERENT morality.
│             |   Mechanically identical, philosophically inverted.
│

       | In-Clan Disciplines cheaper than out-of-Clan (5x vs 7x current).
       | ↣ interesting: the XP cost structure MECHANICALLY encodes Clan identity —
       |   it's cheaper to be what your blood says you should be.
```
